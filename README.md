**Project  Overview**
This project analyzes milk yield patterns across 250000 unique cattle records spanning 15 countries,40breeds and 3 years(2022-2024).The goal was to identify  the key drivers of milk yield in dairy cattle using data analytics  and interactive visualization.

**Tools Used**
-Power BI Desktop - dashboard creation, DAX measures, interactive slicers
-Google Sheets - exploratory data analysis, AVERAGEIF, MAXIFS, MINIFS formulas
-DAX(data Analysis Expressions) - custom measures for Average Milk Yield and Feed Quantity
-Power Query - Data cleaning, column renaming, country name standardization

**Dataset**
-Records:250000 unique cattle
-Variables 37 columns including breed,country,  region, feed type, health scores, vaccine records and milk yield
-Time Period : January 2022 - December 2024
-Farms 1000 unique farmsacross 15 countries

**Key Findings**
-Breed is the dominant driver of milk yield- Holstein Fresian averages 17.51L/day while NDama averages only 2.64L/day, a7 times difference
-Geography barely Matters - average yield varies by less than 0.11L/day across all 15 countries
-Lactation stage significantly affects yield - Early stage cows produce noticeably more than Late stage Cows
-Feed quantity alone does not predict yield - breeds eating similar amounts of feed produce very different yields, confirming genetics as the primary driver
-Weight is Uniform across breeds - all breeds average approximately 500kg, ruling out body size as an explanation for yield differences

**Dashboard Features**
-Interaactive slicers for Breed, Country, Region and Season
-Average Milk Yield by Lactation Stage(column chart)
-Average Milk Yield by Breed(horizontal bar chart)
-Feed Quantity vs Milk Yield by Breed(Scatter chart)
-Average Milk Yield by Country (bar chart)
